  • Actuators: Convert electrical signals into motion.
    • Motors: Rotate joints or wheels.
    • Servos: Enable precise angular motion.
    • Hydraulic Systems: Powerful lifting for heavy robots.
  • Sensors: Enable robots to sense their environment.
    • Ultrasonic: Measures distance.
    • Infrared (IR): Detects proximity.
    • LiDAR: Maps surroundings in 3D.
    • Cameras: For visual input and computer vision.
  • End-Effectors: The "hands" of the robot.
    • Grippers for picking and placing items.
    • Welding tools and suction cups for precision tasks.
  • Kinematics: Study of motion.
    • Forward Kinematics: Position from joint angles.
    • Inverse Kinematics: Joint angles from desired position.
